Phase 6: Field Survey & Data Collection
‚è± Duration: 0.5‚Äì3 days (per site)
This is the core technical work. Surveyors visit the site with professional instruments and capture precise measurements of the property, boundaries, building footprint, individual units, common areas, and elevation data.

üë§ Responsible Roles

Field Surveyor (Lead) Assistant Surveyor

üîß Equipment Used

  • Total Station: Precise angle and distance measurements
  • GNSS/GPS Receiver: Georeferencing and control points
  • Laser Distance Meter: Interior room dimensions
  • Drone (UAV): Aerial mapping for large plots
  • 3D Laser Scanner: Complex building interiors
  • Measuring Tape: Backup measurements
  • Camera: Site photographs for documentation
  • Field Book / Tablet: Recording raw data

‚öôÔ∏è Activities Performed

  • Establish control points using GNSS receiver
  • Set up Total Station and calibrate
  • Survey plot boundaries and verify against affection plan
  • Measure building footprint and setbacks from plot limits
  • Survey each floor: unit boundaries, walls, columns
  • Measure individual unit areas (rooms, bathrooms, balconies, terraces)
  • Survey common areas: corridors, lobbies, stairwells, elevators, etc.
  • Capture elevation data for topographical requirements
  • Take site photographs of all measured areas
  • Record raw field data in field book / tablet
  • Get client representative signature on site visit form
  • Check equipment back in on return to office

üì§ Outputs

  • Raw survey data (coordinates, angles, distances)
  • Site photographs (organized by floor/area)
  • Signed site visit confirmation form
  • Field notes and observations
  • GPS/GNSS coordinate data files

Phase 7: Office Processing, Drafting & Report Preparation
‚è± Duration: 2‚Äì7 days
The raw field data is transferred to the CAD/GIS team who process it into professional survey drawings, area calculations, and reports. This is the most labor-intensive office phase.

üë§ Responsible Roles

CAD Draftsman GIS Specialist Senior Surveyor (guidance)

üõ† Software & Tools Used

  • AutoCAD: 2D floor plans, boundary plans, section drawings
  • AutoCAD Civil 3D: Topographical maps, contour generation
  • ArcGIS / QGIS: Geospatial analysis and mapping
  • Revit / BIM: 3D building models (if required)
  • Trimble Business Center: GNSS data processing
  • Excel: Area calculation spreadsheets
  • 3D Rendering Software: Color cut sheets

‚öôÔ∏è Activities Performed

  • Download and process raw survey data from instruments
  • Import data into AutoCAD / GIS software
  • Draft floor plans for each level with accurate dimensions
  • Calculate individual unit areas (GFA, BUA, sellable area)
  • Delineate common areas vs. private areas
  • Prepare boundary/plot plans with coordinates
  • Create area schedule tables
  • Prepare color cut sheets (rendered floor plans)
  • Generate As-Built drawings comparing actual vs. approved plans
  • Calculate FAR (Floor Area Ratio) for Third-Party Survey
  • Compile the survey report document
  • Format all drawings per authority template/standard

üì§ Outputs / Deliverables

  • AutoCAD floor plans (per floor)
  • Area calculation schedules (per unit, per floor, total)
  • Plot/boundary plan with coordinates
  • Common area plan
  • Color cut sheets
  • As-Built drawings (if applicable)
  • Survey report document (Draft)
  • FAR calculation report (for Third-Party Survey)

Phase 8: Quality Assurance & Internal Review
‚è± Duration: 1‚Äì2 days
Before any submission to authorities, all deliverables go through a rigorous internal quality check by a senior surveyor or QA manager. Errors at this stage caught early prevent costly authority rejections.

üë§ Responsible Roles

QA / Senior Surveyor Project Manager

‚öôÔ∏è Activities Performed

  • Cross-check all area calculations against raw data
  • Verify drawing dimensions match field measurements
  • Ensure drawings comply with authority formatting standards
  • Check that plot boundaries match DLD affection plan
  • Validate unit numbering and nomenclature
  • Verify common area delineation is correct
  • Review survey report for completeness and accuracy
  • Check FAR compliance (for Third-Party Survey)
  • Approve or send back for correction with comments
  • Final sign-off on all deliverables

üì§ Outputs

  • QA approval stamp / sign-off on all documents
  • Correction notes (if any) sent back to CAD team
  • Final approved survey package ready for submission

Phase 9: Authority Submission, Review & Rectification
‚è± Duration: 3‚Äì15 days (authority dependent)
The final survey package is submitted to the relevant authority. The authority reviews the submission and may approve it directly or return it with comments requiring rectification. This loop can repeat multiple times.

üë§ Responsible Roles

Admin / Registration Project Manager CAD Team (for corrections)

‚öôÔ∏è Activities Performed

  • Upload final survey package to authority portal
  • Submit all required hardcopy documents (if needed)
  • Pay submission fees
  • Monitor submission status on authority portal daily
  • Receive authority review comments (if any)
  • Analyze comments and identify required corrections
  • Send corrections back to CAD team for rectification
  • Re-submit corrected documents to authority
  • Follow up with authority for expedited processing
  • Repeat rectification loop until approval received

üîÅ The Rectification Loop (Common)

  • 1st submission ‚Üí Authority comments (30-50% of cases)
  • Correction ‚Üí 2nd submission ‚Üí Usually approved
  • Complex projects may require 3-4 submission rounds
  • Average: 1.5 submissions per project

üì§ Outputs

  • Submission confirmation and reference numbers
  • Authority review comments (documented)
  • Rectification records
  • Final authority approval notification
